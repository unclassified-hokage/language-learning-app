## The 5 Biggest Gaps We're Filling

### Gap 1: AI Conversation Practice — Affordable
**Problem:** Real conversational practice locked behind $30/month (DuolingoMax)
**Our Solution:** Free AI conversation partner powered by open-source LLMs
- Unlimited conversation practice
- Scenario-based (at a café, job interview, meeting family)
- Real-time grammar corrections with explanations
- Pronunciation feedback with phonetic guidance
- Adapts to your level automatically

### Gap 2: Grammar Explained, Not Hidden
**Problem:** Duolingo throws users into exercises without explaining WHY
**Our Solution:** AI-powered grammar explanations triggered at the right moment
- Inline grammar notes that appear when you make a grammar-pattern mistake
- "Why was I wrong?" button after every incorrect answer
- Grammar concepts visualized (e.g., verb conjugation tables, case system diagrams)
- Progressive disclosure — beginners see simple explanations, advanced see full rules

### Gap 3: Real-World Content for Intermediate+ Learners
**Problem:** Duolingo content runs out before fluency. No good app for B1-C1 learners.
**Our Solution:** Curated real-world content at adaptive difficulty
- Simplified news articles, song lyrics, social media posts, movie scenes
- AI-adjusted comprehension questions
- Unknown word highlighting + one-tap translation + SRS integration
- "Learn through media you actually enjoy"

### Gap 4: Pronunciation Coaching
**Problem:** Apps say "right" or "wrong" but never explain HOW to improve pronunciation
**Our Solution:** AI-powered phonetics coaching
- Waveform comparison (your voice vs. native speaker)
- Specific feedback: "Your 'r' is too far back — try positioning tongue here"
- IPA (International Phonetic Alphabet) visual guide for difficult sounds
- Tone coaching for tonal languages (Mandarin, Vietnamese)

### Gap 5: Community Without Chaos
**Problem:** Duolingo closed its forums. HelloTalk/Tandem are unstructured.
**Our Solution:** Structured in-app community
- Language-specific rooms
- "Ask about this lesson" tied to specific lessons/grammar points
- Native speaker verification badges
- Translation exchange (help others with your native language)
- Open source — community can contribute course content

## Target Users

### Primary: "The Frustrated Duolingo User"
- Has been using Duolingo for 3–12 months
- Maintains a streak but can't hold a conversation
- Wants to improve but doesn't know how
- Age: 20–35

### Secondary: "The Serious Self-Learner"
- Uses Anki + multiple apps + YouTube
- Wants everything in one place
- Intermediate-advanced level
- Willing to invest 30+ min/day

### Tertiary: "The Re-starter"
- Has tried learning a language before and quit
- Previous apps felt like homework
- Wants something that fits into daily life
- Age: 30–45
